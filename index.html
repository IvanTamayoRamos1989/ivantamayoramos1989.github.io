Iván Tamayo Ramos works within ACCIONA Corporate Sustainability, contributing regenerative design and AI-enabled process optimization to major infrastructure tenders in the Americas, Europe, and Australasia. He is a 2024 Fellow of the Norman Foster Institute on Sustainable Cities — the inaugural cohort co-directed by Norman Foster and Kent Larson of the MIT Media Lab — and a three-time participant at Bloomberg CityLab (Madrid 2026, Mexico City 2024, Amsterdam 2022).

His practice spans rammed-earth community building in northwest Mexico, the urban regeneration of historic Culiacán, large-scale luxury development in Los Cabos, conservation planning for the Republic of San Marino, and AI-enabled spatial analysis for international infrastructure. He is currently building an AI-powered tool for regenerative and sustainable infrastructure across the project lifecycle.
