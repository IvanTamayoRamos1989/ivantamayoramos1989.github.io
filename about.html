Iván Tamayo Ramos is an architect and regenerative design consultant working at the intersection of infrastructure, ecological restoration, and sustainable finance. He works within ACCIONA Corporate Sustainability, contributing regenerative design and AI-enabled process optimization to major infrastructure tenders in the Americas, Europe, and Australasia, and is building an AI-powered tool for regenerative and sustainable infrastructure across the project lifecycle. Based between Madrid and Mexico City, with more than a decade of practice across Mexico, Spain, and Central Europe.

He is a 2024 Fellow of the Norman Foster Institute on Sustainable Cities — selected to the inaugural cohort co-directed by Norman Foster (founder of Foster + Partners) and Kent Larson (Director of the City Science Group at the MIT Media Lab) — where he co-developed a conservation and sustainable development plan for the Republic of San Marino. He is a three-time participant at Bloomberg CityLab, the global urbanism convening of Bloomberg Philanthropies and the Aspen Institute (Madrid 2026, Mexico City 2024, Amsterdam 2022).

Positioning: a distinct profile

Most sustainability advisors fall into one of three siloes — design without finance, finance without ecology, or ecology without deployment. Iván Tamayo Ramos has built his practice in the opposite direction:

  1. First, the building, in earth and with the community. A decade running Tierradura — rammed-earth, social housing, Fair Trade collaborations.
  2. Then, the development, at scale. Project management of a 400-hectare luxury destination (Costa Palmas, Los Cabos) hosting Four Seasons and Aman, plus the founding of its philanthropic foundation.
  3. Then, the city. Urban regeneration of historic Culiacán with MAPASIN, in collaboration with the Bloomberg Initiative for Global Road Safety.
  4. Then, the region. Inaugural cohort of the Norman Foster Institute on Sustainable Cities; conservation and sustainable development plan for the Republic of San Marino, with Norman Foster, Kent Larson (MIT Media Lab), and Pritzker laureates Alejandro Aravena, Francis Kéré, and Anne Lacaton among the programme faculty.
  5. Now, the infrastructure. Regenerative design and AI-enabled process optimization contributions within ACCIONA Corporate Sustainability, across P3, rail, and energy tenders in three continents — with an AI tool in active development to support the project lifecycle.
From rammed earth in Sinaloa to P3 corridors across three continents — the same problem, at another scale.

Norman Foster Institute on Sustainable Cities — Academic Context

Iván Tamayo Ramos was a member of the inaugural 2024 cohort of the Norman Foster Institute on Sustainable Cities — a 36-week, full-time programme on the Madrid campus, co-directed by Norman Foster and Kent Larson, structured in three stages (Foundations, Transformations, Interventions), with scholars working directly with three European pilot cities and presenting their proposals to the cities' mayors and civic leaders.

Co-Directors

  • Norman Foster — President of the Norman Foster Foundation; Advocate to the United Nations Forum of Mayors; founder and Executive Chairman of Foster + Partners.
  • Kent Larson — Director of the City Science Group at the MIT Media Lab.

Provost & Honorary Dean
